Yuvraj Singh

Yuvraj Singh, a former Indian cricketer, battled cancer during his cricket career. He successfully fought the disease and returned to the sport, showcasing incredible resilience and determination.

Stephen Hawking

The renowned theoretical physicist Stephen Hawking, who suffered from amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), continued to make groundbreaking contributions to the field of cosmology while battling the disease. His work on black holes and the nature of the universe is celebrated worldwide

Nick Vujicic

Nick Vujicic was born in 1982 in Melbourne, Australia, with a rare condition called tetra-amelia syndrome, which left him without arms and legs. Despite his physical limitations, Nick has become a motivational speaker, author, and evangelist who has inspired millions of people worldwide.

Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs, the co-founder of Apple Inc., is known for his significant contributions to the world of technology. He faced his own health challenges during his life: Pancreatic Cancer Diagnosis: In 2003, Jobs was diagnosed with a rare form of pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor, a serious health condition. He chose to keep his diagnosis private initially

Michael J Fox

Michael J. Fox: Actor Michael J. Fox has been living with Parkinson's disease since 1991. Despite his diagnosis, he has continued to act and has become an advocate for Parkinson's research, raising millions of dollars for the cause.

Selena Gomez

Singer and actress Selena Gomez has been open about her struggles with lupus, an autoimmune disease. Despite her health challenges, she has achieved great success in the entertainment industry and has used her platform to raise awareness about lupus

Stevie Wonder

Stevie Wonder is a beloved American icon and an indisputable genius and The legendary musician Stevie Wonder was born prematurely and became blind shortly after birth due to complications. Despite his blindness, he became one of the most influential and successful musicians in history, winning numerous Grammy Awards and creating timeless music.

Manisha Koirala:

Manisha Koirala is a well-known Indian actress who has appeared in numerous Bollywood films. she was both deaf and blind from a young age due to an illness.She overcame these challenges with the help of her teacher,Anne Sullivan,and went on to become a renowned author, political activist, and lecturer, advocating for the rights of people with disabilities.

Christopher Reeve

Best known for his role as Superman in the 1970s and 1980s, Christopher Reeve became a quadriplegic after a horse-riding accident in 1995. He continued to work as an actor and became a vocal advocate for spinal cord injury research.

Dipa Karmakar:

Gymnast Dipa Karmakar faced physical limitations due to flat feet, which is uncommon in gymnastics. However, she worked tirelessly to overcome these challenges and became the first Indian female gymnast to compete in the Olympics, representing India at the 2016 Rio Olympics.
